I am a doctoral researcher at the University of Stavanger working on medical imaging, artificial intelligence, and acute ischemic stroke analysis.

My research explores multimodal CT and MRI analysis, lesion characterization, image registration, feature extraction, and clinically meaningful interpretation with deep learning. I am part of the Safer Healthcare project, a collaboration between the University of Stavanger, Stavanger University Hospital, and Laerdal Medical.

Before starting my PhD, I completed the Erasmus Mundus Joint Master in Marine and Maritime Intelligent Robotics at Instituto Superior Técnico and Université de Toulon, following a B.Sc. in Computer Science from BRAC University. My broader background spans computer vision, robotics, underwater image analysis, LIDAR perception, disaster risk reduction, and applied AI systems.

Research interests:

  • Medical image analysis for acute stroke
  • Trustworthy AI and multimodal learning
  • Image registration, feature extraction, and lesion characterization
  • Computer vision, robotics, LIDAR perception, and underwater imaging

Selected Work

  • Acute ischemic stroke analysis: AI-assisted interpretation of CT and MRI data for clinically relevant stroke assessment.
  • Underwater image recognition: depth-based image augmentation and recognition methods for underwater exploration.
  • Disaster risk reduction: deep learning algorithms for detecting fire and waterlogging from camera footage in slum areas.
  • Autonomous driving perception: object detection, lane detection, distance measurement, and safe-zone decision support.
